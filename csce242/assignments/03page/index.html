Hard Tire

Hard compound tires are the most durable option in Formula 1, designed to last the longest distance during a race. They can withstand higher track temperatures and extended stints without significant performance loss. While they offer less grip than softer compounds, their consistency makes them ideal for endurance strategies where minimizing pit stops is key.

Soft Tire

Soft compound tires are the fastest option, delivering maximum grip and performance. They heat up quickly and are perfect for qualifying laps or short, aggressive stints during a race. However, their high performance comes at the cost of rapid wear, making them unsuitable for long runs but invaluable for gaining time in critical moments.

Medium Tire

Medium compound tires strike a balance between performance and durability. They provide more grip and speed than hard tires while still lasting a reasonable number of laps. Teams often use mediums when they need flexibility in strategy, as they can handle a wide range of track conditions and offer competitive lap times without wearing too quickly.

Ferarri F1 Car 1

Formula 1 cars are the pinnacle of motorsport engineering, built for extreme speed, precision, and performance. They feature lightweight carbon fiber chassis, advanced aerodynamics, and hybrid power units that combine turbocharged internal combustion engines with electric motors. Every component is designed to maximize efficiency and grip, from the sculpted wings that generate downforce to the intricate suspension systems that keep the tires firmly on the track. Capable of exceeding 200 mph and cornering with forces over 5G, F1 cars push the limits of technology and human skill.
